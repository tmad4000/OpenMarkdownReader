Mac app for files on disk

Open any markdown file. Edit it quickly. Leave the file where it lives.

OpenMarkdownReader is for people who want the Obsidian comfort zone without the vault requirement. It opens arbitrary .md and text files, keeps the preview/editor loop fast, and stays local-first by design.

What it is

A reader and editor built around files, not a database.

The goal is simple: keep the markdown workflow familiar, fast, and low-friction while preserving the freedom to open whatever is already on your Mac.

Open anything

Loose files, folders, text notes.

The app is built for real files on disk. That means existing notes, ad hoc docs, scratch files, and the long tail of markdown that never lived in a vault.

Low friction editing

Auto-save when you want it, no ceremony when you do not.

The workflow is designed around quick edits, preview toggles, and keeping your changes in sync without turning every note into a full project.

Wikilink support

Feels familiar to Obsidian users.

Parser-level wikilinks make notes feel connected, while the underlying model stays simple: files, folders, and markdown.

Fast browsing

Sidebar navigation that stays out of the way.

File-tree navigation, multi-tab workflows, and a readable preview pane keep large note sets usable without a lot of UI ceremony.

“Obsidian, but you can open arbitrary files and not be forced into a vault-first workflow.”

That is the positioning in one sentence. The rest of the homepage just explains why that matters: less friction, fewer imports, and a cleaner fit for the markdown files people already have.
